Influence Tactics (in more detail)

LInfluence tactics are strategies that one can use to influence, or change, how others feel or behave around you. There are several influence tactics, including:

  • Rational persuation
  • Inspirational appeal
  • Consultation
  • Ingratiation
  • Personal appeal
  • Exchange
  • Pressure
  • Coalition
Different influence tactics can be used in different situations. For example, if you need your friend to give you a ride to school, you could offer to buy them lunch, using the exchange tactic to influence a successful outcome. On the other hand, if you are feeling malevolent, you could use pressure tactics, threatening to expose your friend’s baby photos to other people if they do not give you a ride.

After learning about influence tactics, Ayden and I realized the different ways in which we could change other’s values, behaviours and beliefs. We learned that there are better influence tactics for certain situations—for example, consultation might be better for raising money (Mr. Sokugawa agrees to match the amount of donations his class raises for Christmas Cheer), while coalition would be more effective (the whole class bands together) to ask for a retest.

Coalition is my favourite influence tactic (no pressure)
