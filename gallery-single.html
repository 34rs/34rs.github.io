LinkedIn was a program we learned to use to network with students and professionals in business contexts. Like other social media platforms, LinkedIn allowed us to connect with those who share similar career interests as ourselves, among mutual connections to further build opportunities.

Ayden Nguyen's LinkedIn

What is LinkedIn? (in more detail)

This year, we discovered the benefits of LinkedIn through our Organizational Behaviour class. LinkedIn is a powerful tool that helps individuals connect with others professionally throughout the world. Despite being a niche topic in the course with more application than conceptual understanding, it helped us expand our potential networking opportunities by establishing an online presence.

  • Allowed us to connect with others with similar interests
  • Provided employment opportunities/workshop experiences
  • Allowed for greater outreach, aligning with our pursuit of extraversion to become more versatile in professional environments

LinkedIn was a good way for us to recollect our experiences/achievements as we added them to our profiles. Collaborating with classmates in this way made us conscious of our holistic profile and how it compared to others. Despite both our profiles being quite complete, seeing how others shaped their experiences to match their interests motivated us to engage in experiences such as volunteering and case competitionsâ€“things that enhanced our interpersonal skills. LinkedIn, for me (Ayden), was my first professional profile page, helping me change from introverted to somewhat outgoing.

In today's online presence, the key to success is LinkedIn online connections.
